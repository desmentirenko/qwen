Abstract
This study analyzes revelations and findings of the Ukrainian trial verdict and other court
decisions concerning killings of two Maidan protesters and wounding of 10 others in
Khmelnytskyi in Ukraine on February 19, 2014. This case of political violence is closely linked
to the Maidan massacre, which led to the overthrow of the Ukrainian government and ultimately
to the war in Donbas, Russian military interventions in Crimea and Donbas, the Russian
annexation of Crimea and conflicts of Russia with Ukraine and the West that Russia escalated by
launching the illegal invasion of Ukraine on February 24, 2022. This paper analyzes the content
of the text of the verdict, which was issued by the Khmelnytskyi court in March 2025 following
a several-year long closed trial, and other court decisions concerning this case from the official
database of court decisions in Ukraine. This verdict and government investigations de facto
confirmed, based on such evidence as videos, forensic ballistic and medical examinations, on-
site investigative experiments, and witness testimonies, that the Maidan activists in
Khmelnytskyi were shot not by the SBU officers but by far-right Maidan activists. The study
compares this to findings of scholarly studies and narratives propagated by governments and the
media and discusses implications.

Previous Studies and the Research Question
This paper analyzes revelations and findings of the declassified Ukrainian trial verdict
in 2025 and other court decisions concerning killings of two Maidan protesters and wounding of
10 others in the city of Khmelnytskyi in Ukraine on February 19, 2014 during Euromaidan. The
research question is what the verdict reveals about which party of the Maidan conflict
perpetrated this case of political violence, which is closely linked to the Maidan massacre in
Kyiv on February 18-20, 2014.
The massacre of the protesters and the police during Euromaidan in Ukraine was a
crucial case of political violence in Ukraine and the world. (See Katchanovski, 2016a, 2020,
2021, 2023a, 2023b, 2024; 2025a, 2025b; Kudelia, 2018). This mass killing resulted in the
overthrow of the Viktor Yanukovych government and ultimately led or contributed to the
annexation of Crimea by Russia, a civil war and Russian military interventions in Donbas, and
conflicts between Ukraine and Russia and the West and Russia. (Black and Johns, 2016;
Katchanovski, 2015, 2016b, 2017, 2025b; Hahn, 2018; Sakwa, 2015). Russia escalated these
conflicts on February 24, 2022, with the illegal invasion and the war with Ukraine, which also
became a proxy war between the West and Russia (Katchanovski, 2022, 2023a, 2025b).
The dominant narrative promoted by the governments and the media in Ukraine and the
West attributed the massacres of the Maidan protesters in both Kyiv and Khmelnytskyi to the
Yanukovych government forces. (Boyd-Barrett, 2016; Katchanovski, 2024). The official
investigation by the Prosecutor General Office of Ukraine (GPU) charged the Berkut police with
the massacre of the Maidan protesters in Kyiv on February 20, 2014 on the orders of President
Viktor Yanukovych and his heads of the Security Service of Ukraine and the Ministry of Internal
Affairs. The GPU arrested and charged the Berkut special company commander and five

The Verdict and Investigations Revelations
The declassified text of the Ukrainian court verdict in March 2025 confirms the false
flag shooting of Maidan activists in Khmelnytskyi during the Maidan massacre. Forensic expert
examination of videos, forensic ballistic and medical examinations, on-site investigative
experiments by government forensic experts, witness testimonies, and investigations by the SBU
the Ukrainian Military Prosecutor Office showed that gunshots made from the SBU veranda,
which was occupied by Maidan activists, killed elderly female Maidan protester and wounded
other Maidan protesters. The verdict acquitted the ex-head of Khmelnytskyi SBU and the SBU
Alfa officer for shooting of these Maidan activists because of the lack of evidence of their
involvement in the shooting and because of such evidence of the false flag shooting.
The 2025 verdict publicly revealed for the first time that soon after the shooting in
2014 the special commission of the Security Service of Ukraine, which was then under control of
the Maidan leaders, determined that “that the burst of shots from automatic weapons that rang
out on February 19, 2014 at 12:36 p.m. and as a result of which some civilians who were near the
administrative building were injured, was fired from the vestibule of the central entrance of the
Department, where at that time unidentified individuals from among the aggressive protesters
were located.” (Vyrok, 2025).

Conclusion
The declassified Ukrainian court verdict and investigations confirm the false-flag killing
and wounding of Maidan protesters in Khmelnytskyi with far-right involvement on February 19,
2014. The declassified text of the verdict, which was issued by the Khmelnytskyi court in March
2025 following a several-year long closed trial, and the Security Service of Ukraine (SBU) and
Military Prosecutor Office investigations de facto confirmed, based on such evidence as videos,
forensic ballistic and medical examinations, on-site investigative experiments, and witness
testimonies, that an elderly female Maidan protester was killed and three others wounded not by
the SBU officers from inside of the SNU headquarters but by a Maidan activist in the Maidan-

Security Service of Ukraine headquarters in the Khmelnytskyi Region. This is consistent with the
video content analysis showing that the protesters were shot from the SBU veranda and that it
was occupied then by Maidan activists.
Similarly to the verdict and government investigation findings concerning the Maidan
massacre in Kyiv on February 20, 2014, the Ukrainian trial verdict and Ukrainian government
investigations also corroborate findings of previous scholarly studies of this shooting and the
Maidan massacre in Ukraine. They show that the narratives propagated by the Ukrainian and
Western governments and the media about these crucial and closely related cases of political
violence in Ukraine were false.
The verdict by the Khmelnytskyi court also shows that the investigation by of the
Khmelnytskyi shooting by the same special department of Prosecutor General Office (GPU) that
investigated Maidan massacre was falsified and that the regional SBU commander and the SBU
Alfa officer were false accused by the GPU for this deadly murder on trumped-up charges and
that evidence against them was fabricated.
The determination by the Prosecutor General Office of Ukraine investigation
That a handgun bullet that killed another Maidan activist did not match SBU handguns and the
failure to charge anyone with his murder and wounding of other Maidan activist who were shot
while Khmelnytskyi SBU building was set on fire by far-right activists suggest that this also was
a false-flag shooting.
This verdict and investigations also confirm the cover-up by the Turchynov, Poroshenko,
and Zelensky governments and the GPU and SBU of the false-flag shooting and Maidan activists
and leaders, in particular, the far-right who were involved in the killings and wounding of

Maidan massacre, the investigation “lost” key evidence, such as a bullet which wounded one of
the Maidan activists in Khmelnytskyi.
The declassified text of the verdict and SBU and Military Prosecutor Office investigations
also highlight the role of the false-flag Maidan violence and the radical nationalist and neo-Nazi
far-right in the origins of the violent conflicts in Ukraine and conflicts between Ukraine and

in Donbas, and ultimately the ongoing Russia-Ukraine war following the Russian invasion of
